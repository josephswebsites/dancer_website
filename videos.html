Welcome to my video collection!

Click on each title to find a description and link to watch. Do not distribute without my permission.

Please note that I have no intention of copyright infringement and provide these videos for "fair use" only. Thus, I have given credit to the various choreographers, performers, and composers. I have also shortened most of the videos to 30 seconds in length.

These videos are worthwhile to me and I hope they are for you. Enjoy! 

Loss;Love  is Joseph's Dance for Camera, which explores hearing loss and longing for loved ones who have passed. The work exhibits Joseph's modern choreographic innovations and was a successfull collaboration with fine arts peers - Cassie Brewer, Rheid Wilson, and Max Reist.
In 2017,  Joseph took a Men's Ballet class at Western and learned the male variation from Le Corsaire  to the music of Adolphe Adam.  Follow the link and go to 6:56 to see Joseph dance in unison with classmate Will Brighton.
Mojo is a solo that was choreographed and performed by Joseph for a Noon Dance Showing. At the time he was a member of Hip Hop ConnXion and was exploring improvised club/house steps. In addition, he was embodying his witty personality as a nerdy athlete trying to make his move. Note: Joseph was going to wear shoes but was advised not to because of the new floor panels
Bermuda Triangle,  performed to "Take Five" by David Brubeck, was choreographed for a class at Western. In this piece, Joseph enhances his natural way of moving and challenges himself to do the uncommon. He also investigates the shape of a triangle through movement.
Codependent  is a solo that was choreographed and performed by Joseph for a Noon Dance Showing. Inspired by personal experiences and the song "Figures" by Jessie Reyez, Joseph displays the ups and downs of a codependent relationship.
God Knows Why  is a solo choreographed for a class at Western. Even though it is an informal piece, it conveys Joseph's musical upbringings, his religious perseverance, and a mixture of dance styles.
Splash  was a solo choreographed for a class at Western.  The work is based off of a haiku by Matsuo Basho which illustrates "An old silent pond...A frog jumps into the pond, Splash! Silence again."  By analyzing the meaning and syntax of this poem, Joseph creates original choreography to enhance the imagery and rhythm within.
Sarah Jones choreographed I Am Still a Person  with respect to a family member suffering from Alzheimer's.  Her cast, Darion Mayrand, Alyssa Boone, Mayla Roque-Coleman, Kami Miller, Joseph Grossberg, and Jontae McCroy depict members of Jones' family and the struggles they faced. I Am Still a Person  is a staged performance that was presented in the Fall Showcase of 2015.
State of Mind  was Joseph's first solo that he choreographed and performed at a Noon Dance Showing.  To a short cut of the song "New York State of Mind" by Billy Joel,  Joseph relates the vibe of living in New York to his life as a Bronco living in the dorms.
Before coming to Western,  Joseph performed Sleepless  during the White Mountain Summer Dance Festival at Sarah Lawrence College.  The work was co-choreographed by Ian Spak to the music of Oliver Tank.
Choreographed by Ted Hershey and staged by Paul Dennis for the White Lake Summer Dance Festival, Between Us Glimmering  is a celebration between villagers.  The cast consists of Joseph Grossberg, Ashley Pierre-Louis, Kalyan Sayre, Ian Spak, and Maytte Subirana.  The work is performed to the Bulgarian State Radio & Television Female Vocal Choir.
Trial Run  was a staged performance for the White Mountain Summer Dance Festival at Sarah Lawrence College.  Jacoby Pruitt, in collaboration with Kalyan Sayre, Ian Spak, Natalie Zamora, Maytte Subirana, Joseph Grossberg, and Alexandra Caffrey, choreograph with regards to a range of dynamics and talents.  Trial Run  is performed to "Good Enough" by XXYYXX and "Bank Head" by Kingdom featuring Kelela.
