Personal Artistic Statement

4/19/19

    I dance to discover the unknown, and in so doing I hope to positively influence myself, my family, my friends, the field of dance, and society.

    When I was young, I wanted to express myself for the people I love, even if that meant dancing in the basement at 2 AM. Now, I continue to do that but with a wider scope of technical and choreographic possibilities. As a performer, I use emotional anecdotes to tell a story, or I convey an abstract idea. I move with a sense of theatricality, musicality, and the element of “the cool”. My aesthetic, a collection of modern, jazz, and hip hop feels natural and brings joy to my audience, and sometimes provokes interpretation. On the other hand, my aesthetic continues to evolve. I often find new ways of moving; I videotape myself improvising and sometimes I take parts of what I see for choreography.

    In the field of dance, I challenge myself constantly and I am determined to succeed. I am fascinated by the power of dance to entertain like Michael Jackson, to explore like Steve Paxton and Moses Pendleton, to simplify like Erick Hawkins and Trisha Brown, or to promote social change like Katherine Dunham and Bill T. Jones. I find inspiration from my daily life or from the cultures of others and then emphasize well-being, artistic freedom, deeper understanding, and social justice through movement on a grandeur scale. When I graduate, I hope to dance for companies that have the same purpose, such as the Bill T. Jones & Arnie Zane Dance Company, or tour with companies such as MOMIX that have a wide range of repertoire.

Joseph Grossberg
